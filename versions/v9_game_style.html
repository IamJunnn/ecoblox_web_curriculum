The Mezzanine is located at the very top of the Studio window. Think of it as your mission control center! Follow these steps to explore it.

  • 1

    Open Roblox Studio

    Launch Roblox Studio from your desktop or download it from create.roblox.com

The 3D Viewport
üîí Locked
‚è±Ô∏è 6 minutes
‚≠ê Easy

The 3D Viewport is the big area in the center of your screen - this is where the magic happens! It's your window into the 3D world you're creating. Follow these steps to learn the controls.

  • 1

    Find the 3D Viewport

    Look at the large center area of Roblox Studio. This is where you'll see your 3D game world

  • 2

    Practice moving with W, A, S, D keys

    Hold W to move forward, A to move left, S to move backward, D to move right. Try it!

  • 3

    Try rotating your view

    Right-click and drag your mouse to rotate the camera and look around

  • Use the F key to focus

    Click on any object in your scene, then press F to focus your camera on it

    Open the Toolbox

    Go to View menu ‚Üí Toolbox, or look for the Toolbox panel (usually on the left side of Studio)

‚è±Ô∏è 5 minutes
‚≠ê Easy

The Explorer shows you everything in your game organized in a tree structure. Think of it like a file explorer for your game! Follow these steps to explore it.

  • 1

    Find the Explorer panel

    Look on the right side of Studio. The Explorer shows a tree structure of everything in your game

  • 2

    Expand the "Workspace" folder

    Click the arrow next to "Workspace" to see all objects in your 3D world

  • 3

    Click on an object in the Explorer

    Try clicking on "Baseplate" or any other object. Notice it gets selected in the 3D Viewport too!

  • 4

    Explore other services

    Check out "Lighting" for environment settings, and "ReplicatedStorage" for shared resources

The Properties Panel
üîí Locked
‚è±Ô∏è 5 minutes
‚≠ê Easy

The Properties panel is where you customize everything! When you select an object, all its properties appear here. Follow these steps to explore it.

  • 1

    Find the Properties panel

    Look on the right side of Studio, usually below the Explorer. If you can't see it, go to View ‚Üí Properties

  • 2

    Select an object

    Click on the Baseplate (or any part) in the 3D Viewport or Explorer. Watch the Properties panel fill with options!

  • 3

    Try changing a property

    Find "BrickColor" in the Properties panel. Click the color box and choose a new color. Your object changes instantly!

  • 4

    Explore other properties

    Try changing "Material" (wood, metal, etc.) and "Transparency" (0 = solid, 1 = invisible)

The Properties panel - customize everything - Click to zoom in!

‚ö†Ô∏è Watch Out - Common Mistakes

  • Properties panel is empty? Make sure you have an object selected in the Explorer or 3D Viewport
  • Can't find a property? Use the search box at the top of the Properties panel to filter properties
